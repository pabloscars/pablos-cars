What Is a Salvage or Rebuilt Title?

No scare tactics, no sales pitch — just what these titles actually mean.

Salvage Title

An insurance company decided a vehicle was a "total loss" — the cost to repair it was close to or more than what it was worth. A salvage-titled car legally can't be driven until it's repaired and inspected. This is what most of my cars carry.

Rebuilt Title

Once a salvage vehicle is repaired and passes a state inspection confirming it's safe to drive, the title upgrades to "Rebuilt." Damage fixed, cleared to drive.

Why Cars Get Salvage Titles

Often nothing to do with how well the car drives:

Cosmetic damage

Fender-benders and dings expensive to repaint — doesn't touch the engine or frame.

Hail damage

Dents from a storm. The car runs exactly the same as before the storm.

Theft recovery

Stolen, insurance paid the claim, then police recovered it — often in good shape.

Buyer disclaimer: Always do your own inspection — or have a trusted mechanic do one — and confirm you understand a vehicle's title status before you buy. I'll answer any question honestly, but the final decision and inspection are yours.
